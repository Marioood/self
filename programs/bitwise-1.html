These are graphs of 16 bitwise operations that use a truth table. The parameters for each operation are the x and y coordinates of the images. The truth tables are generated by corresponding the first 4 bits of a number to the outputs of a truth table:

a b c
0 0 = 0th bit
0 1 = 1st bit
1 0 = 2nd bit
1 1 = 3rd bit

Hence why there are only 16 operations. (24 = 16)

These graphs and tables may help give a visual proof of De Morgan's Law, some graphs just extract the x and y coordinates from the image, and the AND and OR graphs can be rotated by just moving one of the output bits.
